A patient presented with a complex medical history including herniated discs, shingles, and phrenic nerve injury. The patient was subsequently diagnosed with malignant pleural mesothelioma (MPM), accompanied by pleural lesions and pain. Further investigation revealed right adrenal metastasis. The patient continued to experience pleural lesions and pain. The patient developed atrophy and paralysis of the left oblique abdominal muscles. The patient experienced a recurrence of shingles. The patient was also diagnosed with diabetic neuropathy. The patient's malignant pleural mesothelioma (MPM) progressed, leading to pain, respiratory distress, tumor invasion, and rib metastasis (destruction).
