A 68-year-old male with no family history presented to the First Affiliated Hospital of Nanchang University (Jiangxi, China) in August 2021, complaining of epigastric pain. An abdominal computed tomography (CT) showed a space-occupying lesion in the body of the pancreas. By May 2022, the patient progressed with the development of a posterior cardiac annular nodule (1.7x1.3 cm) with CA199, which was 35.73 U/ml. Consequently, the patient was diagnosed with recurrent metastatic PDAC and had an Eastern Cooperative Oncology Group (ECOG) score of 1.
