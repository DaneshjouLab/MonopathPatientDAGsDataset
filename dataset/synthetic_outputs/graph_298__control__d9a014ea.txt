A 32-year-old female patient was admitted to the hospital after a physical examination revealed nodules in the right upper lung for 1 week. She was diagnosed with Pulmonary inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor (IMT), a rare borderline tumor with the potential for malignant behavior including invasion of surrounding tissues, distant metastasis, and recurrence. Following multidisciplinary consultation, the patient underwent Single-port thoracoscopic lobectomy. Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor (IMT) is a kind of soft tissue tumor that commonly occurs in children and young adults.
