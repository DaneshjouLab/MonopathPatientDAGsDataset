A patient presented with anaplastic lymphoma kinase (ALK)-positive adenocarcinoma of the lung. Initial findings included a lesion in the right upper lobe, enlarged hilar and mediastinal lymph nodes, and multiple mediastinal lymph nodes invasion, indicative of mediastinum-invasive NSCLC. The patient also exhibited mild edema of the lower limbs and grade 1 hepatic damage. The patient underwent treatment leading to pathological tumor downstaging. However, the patient experienced recurrence. Throughout the treatment course, the patient experienced adverse events. The patient also had hilar and mediastinal lymph node metastasis.
