Patients presented with a range of manifestations including respiratory distress, fever, and obstructive shock. Preoperative symptoms included dyspnea, respiratory distress, hemoptysis, and chest pain. Clinical examination focused on detecting signs of respiratory distress and manifestations of metastasis such as weight loss and neurologic complains. Two cases presented with a mediastinal mass, while the other two presented with pneumothorax. Recurrence was detected in a type II case approximately 2 years after the operation. Another type II case developed brain metastasis, which was discovered 3 years after the operation.
